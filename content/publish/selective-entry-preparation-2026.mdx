Preparing for selective entry exams requires a clear plan, regular practice, and steady confidence.

This guide outlines the main areas students should focus on and the study habits that can make preparation more effective in 2026.

## Core Areas

### Mathematics
Mathematics often requires both speed and accuracy. Students should strengthen their understanding of number skills, algebra, geometry, and problem-solving strategies through regular practice.

### English
English preparation should include reading comprehension, vocabulary building, and clear writing. Reading high-quality articles and practising written responses can help improve both understanding and expression.

### Science
Science revision should focus on core ideas in physics, chemistry, and biology, as well as scientific reasoning. Reviewing concepts and applying them through questions can build confidence.

### General Ability
General ability questions test logical thinking, pattern recognition, and reasoning. Consistent exposure to these question types can help students become more comfortable with them over time.

## Study Approach

### Staying Organised
A structured timetable can help students balance subjects and revise consistently. Breaking study into manageable sessions often works better than leaving everything until the last minute.

### Practising Under Pressure
Mock exams are useful because they help students experience time limits and test conditions. They can also highlight strengths and areas that need more attention.

### Learning From Mistakes
Reviewing incorrect answers is one of the best ways to improve. Each mistake can show where understanding needs to be strengthened.

## Final Thoughts

Selective entry preparation is not only about working hard, but also about working wisely.

With regular effort, careful review, and a positive mindset, students can build the confidence they need to perform at their best.
