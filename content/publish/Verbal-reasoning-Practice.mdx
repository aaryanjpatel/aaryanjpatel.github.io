Preparing for selective entry exams made me realise that Verbal Reasoning (VR) is not just about logic—it depends heavily on vocabulary. This section shares how I improved my VR scores by building a strong habit of learning new words every day.

📖 My VR Improvement Journey

At the beginning, I struggled with many VR questions because the words were obscure and unfamiliar.
Synonyms, antonyms, and analogies were especially difficult, and I often relied on guessing.
This made my accuracy low and slowed me down in timed tests.

🧠 Strategy I Used
1. Daily Vocabulary Habit

I started learning a small number of words every day. Instead of rushing through long lists, I focused on consistency and made sure I didn’t skip days.

2. Deep Understanding

For each word, I learned:

All meanings (if there were multiple)
Synonyms and antonyms in VR style
A proper sentence to understand usage

This helped me truly understand the word instead of just memorising it.

3. Regular Revision

Revision was key. At first, many words stayed latent in my memory, but by revising daily, they became easy to recall during tests.

4. Quality Over Quantity

I took a meticulous approach—learning fewer words but mastering them completely rather than rushing through many.

📈 Results I Saw
Faster solving of synonym and analogy questions
Better understanding of passages
Improved accuracy under time pressure
Increased confidence in VR sections


🔑 Key Takeaways
Consistency matters more than intensity
Understanding is better than memorising
Revision turns weak memory into strong recall
A small daily habit leads to big improvement
